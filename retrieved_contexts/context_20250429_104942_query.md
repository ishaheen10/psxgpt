# Profit And Loss Account

The Bank reported a Profit after Tax of Rs 101.5 billion, reflecting a 20% increase from Rs 84.5 billion in the prior year.

The basic Earnings Per Share rose by 20%, reaching Rs 56.62 per share, compared to Rs 47.18 per share in 2023.

The Bank’s net spread grew by 27%, primarily driven by strong balance sheet growth. This was further bolstered by a notable increase in average current and savings account balances, contributing to a healthy net spread. Fee, commission, and other income rose by 20% year-on-year, with a significant uptick in fee income from debit cards. As a result, the Bank’s non-funded income saw a remarkable growth of 31%, reaching Rs 29 billion, up from Rs 22 billion in 2023.

Operating and other expenses increased to Rs 84.6 billion, up from Rs 71.8 billion, primarily due to higher costs associated with the opening of 47 new branches and investments in IT infrastructure. Despite this, the Bank remained focused on cost optimization through operational efficiencies and process streamlining, leading to an improvement in its income efficiency ratio, which decreased to 27% from 29% in the previous year. The total tax charge for the year rose to Rs 120.6 billion, compared to Rs 84.9 billion, driven by higher profitability.
